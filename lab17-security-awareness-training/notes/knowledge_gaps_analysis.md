# Knowledge Gap Analysis — Phishing Training

## Observations from Quiz (Hypothetical Team Results)
- Many participants trusted emails that looked “professional” (logos, formatting).
- Several participants believed replying to the sender to “confirm” is safe.
- Some participants were unclear on what to do AFTER clicking a suspicious link.
- A few participants did not realize phishing can occur via SMS/calls (smishing/vishing).

## Common Mistakes (Mapped to Quiz Questions)
- Q1: Visual branding mistaken as trust proof.
- Q2/Q4: Verification methods weak (replying to email instead of trusted channel).
- Q6/Q9: Incident response steps missing (reporting, password change, session revoke).
- Q8: Limited understanding of phishing channels beyond email.

## Recommended Improvements for Next Training
1) Add more real screenshots of phishing emails and look-alike domains.
2) Add a short “What to do after you click” checklist slide (bigger font).
3) Add a mini demo: hovering links + checking headers (basic).
4) Run follow-up quiz in 2 weeks to measure improvement.
