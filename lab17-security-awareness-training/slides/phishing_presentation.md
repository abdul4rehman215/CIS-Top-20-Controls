# Phishing: A Deceptive Cyber Threat

## Slide 1 — Introduction
- **Title:** Phishing: A Deceptive Cyber Threat
- **Goal:** Understand how phishing works and how to stop it.
- **Why it matters:** One click can lead to account takeover, malware, or data loss.

---

## Slide 2 — What is Phishing?
- Phishing is a type of cyberattack where attackers pretend to be trusted sources.
- The goal is to trick users into:
  - sharing passwords/OTP
  - clicking malicious links
  - downloading malware attachments

---

## Slide 3 — How Phishing Works (Attack Flow)
**Diagram (text-based flow):**
1) Attacker crafts fake email/SMS →  
2) Victim receives message →  
3) Victim clicks link/opens attachment →  
4) Victim enters credentials or installs malware →  
5) Attacker uses stolen data for fraud/access

---

## Slide 4 — Common Phishing Techniques
- **Urgency:** “Your account will be locked in 30 minutes”
- **Authority:** “This is IT Admin / HR”
- **Fear:** “Unusual login detected”
- **Reward:** “You won a gift card”
- **Look-alike domains:** paypaI.com (capital i) vs paypal.com

---

## Slide 5 — Real-World Examples
- Fake bank verification emails
- “Invoice attached” emails (malware payload)
- Fake password reset notifications from Microsoft/Google
- “Your delivery failed” SMS with a link

---

## Slide 6 — Red Flags to Spot Phishing
- Sender email doesn’t match the company domain
- Strange spelling/grammar
- Unexpected attachments or urgent link requests
- Hovering shows a different URL than text displays
- Requests for OTP, password, or personal information

---

## Slide 7 — Best Practices to Mitigate Risk
- Verify requests using a trusted channel (call/Teams)
- Hover links before clicking
- Don’t open unexpected attachments
- Use MFA but **never share OTP**
- Report suspicious emails to security/IT

---

## Slide 8 — What to Do If You Clicked
- Disconnect from network (if malware suspected)
- Change password immediately (from a clean device)
- Notify IT/Security
- Check account activity and revoke sessions
- Run endpoint scan if available

---

## Slide 9 — Summary
- Phishing targets human trust, not just systems.
- Slow down, verify, and report.
- Security is everyone’s responsibility.
